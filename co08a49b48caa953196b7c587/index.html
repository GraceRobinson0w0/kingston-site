Visit Kingston upon Thames

Enjoy the culture and cuisine in this vibrant borough close to the center of London.

Top three activities to do in Kingston

Take a stroll through the market

Kingston upon Thames has been home to a bustling market for centuries with the oldest surviving records dating back to the early 1200s, and Kingston’s historical market is still thriving today.

Have a picnic in the park

Its ancient woods, rolling hills and wide-open grasslands are bursting with life – from historic herds of deer to birds, invertebrates, fungi and wildflowers. It’s no wonder that the park is London’s largest Site of Special Scientific Interest and a National Nature Reserve.

Enjoy an evening show

Founded by Sir Peter Hall, and modelled on the original Elizabethan Rose Theatre on London’s Bankside, Rose Theatre is the largest producing theatre in South West London.

Top three resturants to do in Kingston

KOKORO

Kokoro is a Korean Japanese restaurant. It is a bright and vibrant venue for sushi and street food, handmade all day, served quickly and with no fuss.

Poor Boys

Small & unfussy quick-serve eatery offering New Orlean po' boys sandwiches & dessert.

Riverside Vegetaria

Vegetarian, vegan and gluten/wheat-free dishes, available outdoors by the riverbank in summer.
